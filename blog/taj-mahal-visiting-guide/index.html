Taj Mahal Visiting Guide — Timings, Tickets, Tips & How to Reach

Everything you need to know before visiting the Taj Mahal — entry fees, opening hours, best time of day, what to carry, and how to get there from Agra station. Updated for 2026.

Padma Shree TravelsApril 202612 min read

Quick Facts — Taj Mahal

Location: Agra, Uttar Pradesh

Timings: Sunrise to Sunset (closed Fridays)

Ticket: ₹50 Indian  |  ₹1,100 Foreign

Best Time: October–March, at sunrise

From Agra Station: ~5 km (15 min by taxi)

The Taj Mahal is one of the most visited monuments in the world and a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Built by Emperor Shah Jahan between 1632 and 1653 in memory of his wife Mumtaz Mahal, this white marble masterpiece attracts over 7 million visitors every year.

Taj Mahal Timings

The Taj Mahal opens 30 minutes before sunrise and closes 30 minutes before sunset, every day except Friday. On Fridays, the monument is closed for prayers at the mosque inside the complex.

In practical terms, gates open around 6:00 AM in winter and 5:30 AM in summer. Night viewing is available on full moon nights from 8:30 PM to 12:30 AM in batches of 50 visitors.

Best Time to Visit Taj Mahal

October to March is the ideal season. Temperatures are pleasant (10–25°C) and the marble glows in soft winter sunlight. Sunrise is the absolute best time of day — the Taj appears to change colour from pink to gold to white. Crowds are also thinnest at this hour.

Avoid May–June when temperatures exceed 45°C and the marble platform becomes too hot to stand on. Read our detailed guide on the best time to visit Taj Mahal for month-by-month advice.

How to Reach Taj Mahal from Agra Station

The Taj Mahal is approximately 5 km from Agra Cantonment Railway Station, about 15–20 minutes by road. The most comfortable way is a private taxi. You can book a full-day Agra sightseeing cab from ₹2,500 that picks you up at the station and covers all monuments.

Other options include auto-rickshaw (₹100–150) and e-rickshaw (₹50–80). No vehicles are allowed within 500 metres of the Taj — you walk or take a battery bus (₹10) from the parking area.

Entry Gates — Which to Use

  • East Gate — Most popular. Shortest queue at sunrise. Recommended for early visits.
  • West Gate — Good alternative. Closer to some hotels.
  • South Gate — Least crowded but farthest from the monument.

What to Carry & Not Carry

Carry These

  • Valid photo ID (Aadhaar, Passport)
  • Mobile phone and small camera
  • Water bottle, sunscreen, comfortable shoes

Not Allowed Inside

  • Tripods, selfie sticks, power banks, earphones
  • Food, tobacco, large bags, sharp objects

Free lockers are available near all three entry gates.
