Complete Mathura Vrindavan Cab from Agra — One Day Pilgrimage

The Mathura Vrindavan tour from Agra is one of the most popular pilgrimage day trips in North India. Both Mathura (birthplace of Lord Krishna) and Vrindavan (where he spent his childhood) are located just 60–75 km from Agra, making it an easy and comfortable day trip by taxi.

Padma Shree Travels offers a complete Agra Mathura Vrindavan taxi package that covers both cities in a single trip. Our experienced drivers plan the route to maximise temple visits while ensuring you don’t feel rushed. Start early, visit the most important temples, and return to Agra by evening — all in the comfort of a clean, air-conditioned car.

Tour Itinerary: Agra → Mathura → Vrindavan → Agra

Part 1 — Mathura (Morning)

Your driver picks you up at 6:00–7:00 AM. The drive to Mathura takes about 1.5 hours. In Mathura you visit:

🏭 Krishna Janmabhoomi

Lord Krishna’s birthplace — the most sacred site in Mathura. Includes the main temple and the underground prison cell where he was born.

🏛 Dwarkadhish Temple

One of the finest temples in Mathura, known for intricate carvings, vibrant festivals, and a lively devotional atmosphere.

🌊 Vishram Ghat

The sacred ghat on the Yamuna where Lord Krishna rested after defeating Kansa. Beautiful morning prayers and atmosphere.

Part 2 — Vrindavan (Afternoon)

After Mathura, your driver takes you to Vrindavan (15 km away, ~25 minutes). In Vrindavan you visit:

🏭 Banke Bihari Temple

The most famous temple in Vrindavan. The unique “jhanki” darshan — where the curtain is periodically drawn — is a soul-stirring experience.

✨ Prem Mandir

Magnificent white marble temple. The evening light show illuminating the entire structure is breathtaking.

🏛 ISKCON Temple

The grand Krishna Balaram Mandir. Peaceful atmosphere, beautiful deity worship, and excellent prasadam.

🌿 Nidhivan & Seva Kunj

Mysterious sacred groves associated with Krishna’s Raas Leela. No one is allowed after sunset.

🌊 Kesi Ghat

Sacred bathing ghat on the Yamuna. The evening aarti ceremony is deeply moving.

🏛 Radha Vallabh Temple

One of the oldest temples in Vrindavan, dating to 1585. Deep devotional significance and unique architecture.

After visiting Vrindavan’s temples, your driver takes you back to Agra. You typically reach by 6:00–7:00 PM, depending on time spent at each temple.

Tips for Your Mathura Vrindavan Tour

Start early (6–7 AM) to make the most of your day. Wear comfortable walking shoes. Carry a small bag for offerings. Many temples have a no-phone policy — your driver can keep your phone safe in the car. Dress modestly for temple visits. If visiting in summer, carry water — the AC car provides a cool, restful space between stops.
