Temples & Places

What You’ll Visit

🏛

Krishna Janmabhoomi, Mathura

The birth site of Lord Krishna. The main temple complex contains the prison cell (Garbha Griha) where Krishna was born. One of the most visited pilgrimage sites in North India, drawing lakhs of devotees year-round.

🏛

Dwarkadhish Temple, Mathura

A grand 19th-century Vaishnava temple near Vishram Ghat on the Yamuna. Famous for its elaborate festivals, particularly Janmashtami and Holi celebrations. The main idol is a striking black marble form of Krishna as Dwarkadheesh.

🏛

Banke Bihari Mandir, Vrindavan

The most beloved temple in Vrindavan. The idol of Banke Bihari (a playful form of Krishna) is considered extraordinarily sacred. The curtain (parda) tradition makes darshan here unique – the deity’s full gaze is considered too intense to bear.

🏛

Prem Mandir, Vrindavan

A modern white-marble temple built by Jagadguru Kripalu Maharaj, opened in 2012. Intricately carved exterior scenes from Radha-Krishna’s life. Especially beautiful in the evening when the temple is lit up – arrival by late afternoon is recommended.

🏛

ISKCON Temple, Vrindavan

The Krishna-Balarama Mandir on Bhaktivedanta Swami Marg, one of the finest ISKCON temples in India. Peaceful atmosphere, clean premises, and a spiritual program that runs through the day. Easy to visit before or after Banke Bihari.

🏛

Radha Rani Temple, Barsana

Radha’s birthplace village, ~45 km from Vrindavan. The Laddli Ji (Radha Rani) temple sits on top of Brahmagiri hill and is reached by stairs with views across the Braj plains. Barsana is world-famous for Lathmar Holi – the only place where women traditionally play Holi with sticks.

Itinerary

Suggested Route Flow

A typical full-day tour from Agra follows this sequence – timings are approximate and adjusted based on traffic and your temple preferences:

  • 6:00–6:30 AM — Pickup from your hotel or address in Agra
  • 7:30–8:00 AM — Arrive Mathura (~55 km via NH-19/Yamuna Expressway). Start with Vishram Ghat, then Krishna Janmabhoomi and Dwarkadhish. Allow ~2 hours for Mathura darshan.
  • 10:00–10:30 AM — Drive to Vrindavan (~15 km, ~30 min). Begin with Banke Bihari Mandir, then ISKCON Krishna-Balarama temple, then Prem Mandir. Allow ~2.5–3 hours for Vrindavan.
  • 1:00–1:30 PM — Drive to Barsana (~45 km, ~1 hour). Lunch stop en route if needed. Climb to Radha Rani temple. Allow ~1.5 hours at Barsana.
  • 3:30–4:00 PM — Depart Barsana for Agra (~90 km, ~1.5–2 hours via Kosi Kalan–Hathras road or NH-19 via Vrindavan bypass)
  • 7:00–8:00 PM — Return to Agra

The driver waits at every stop for as long as you need. You can skip or add temples without any change in fare.
